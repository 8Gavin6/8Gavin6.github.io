Snooker World Top 10
Snooker World Top 10 Ronnie Antonio O'Sullivan is a professional snooker player from England, the reigning world champion and currently ranked number one in the world. He is also the holder of quite a few records in snooker, including being tied with Stephen Hendry for the most career-era world titles with seven. He is also widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the sport.
Mark Allen, is a professional snooker player from Northern Ireland. He won the World Amateur Championship in 2004. The following year, he entered the main tour and reached the elite top 16 after only three seasons. A prolific record breaker, Allen has hit over 400 breaks in the professional game. Currently ranked 10th in the world, Allen reached his first ranking event final at the 2011 British Championship.
He has a great scoring ability (often 80+ in a single shot), a long pot success rate with high accuracy, and has made 901 single shots and 7 perfect scores (147) as of 2022. He is the world's third-best scorer (Ronnie O'Sullivan's 1,107 and John Higgins' 900), and they are the only three snooker players to have 800+ career bogeys at the 2021 World Championship. He is a serial scorer, who set the world record for "100+ in a single season" with 102 in 19-20, and is second in the world with 90 in 20-21, a record (90+ centuries in a single season) that no player on the Conference roster has yet achieved. He was then beaten by John Higgins in the final of the World Championship, finishing as runner-up. At the end of the year, he defeated Allen 10-8 in the final of the British Championship to win that year's event. Before his current No. 1 ranking, he briefly reached No. 1 in the world rankings for the first time in November 2012 after winning the International Championship.
